Our English for Academic Purposes (EAP) courses prepare you to succeed at an English-speaking high school, college or university. Our agreements with 65 universities and colleges across Canada and the USA will put you on the path to academic success.

We offer the same program in Calgary. Compare our 2 Canadian campuses or explore EAP at GV Victoria below.

Program aims and focus

Our EAP program focuses on strengthening general language skills, especially in communication and academic reading and writing at an advanced level. The program also covers essay writing, research papers, study skills, note-taking, presentations and research.

Your skills will be developed through one or more from a set of nine modules. We can help you choose which options are best for you.

Elective modules

  1. Vocabulary and grammar essentials
  2. Gateway to academic writing
  3. Reading and discussion
  4. Speaking strategies for success
  5. Reading and writing
  6. Listening and speaking
  7. Conversation and vocabulary on global issues
  8. Canadian culture and society
  9. English for social media and digital literacy

EAP program format

To ensure a successful transfer to your chosen college or university, the program includes regular scheduled meetings with your Pathway Advisor to review your academic progress and offer help with the application process.

Our intensive programs run throughout the year, so you may choose any start date that suits you. The process begins with a placement test to find out the length of study you need to achieve your pathway goal. And you’ll then follow a carefully designed structure.

Program structure

  • Lessons per week: 30
    • 50 minutes per lesson
    • 20 morning lessons of general communication
    • 10 afternoon lessons of academic pathway electives
  • Schedule: Monday to Friday
  • Delivery options:
    • Face-to-face
    • Virtual: attendance online via our GV Live platform
    • Hybrid: a combination of face-to-face and virtual
  • Ages: 16+ and over
  • Program start:

    Programs start with a fresh intake of students every Monday throughout the year (exc. Canadian public holidays).

Language levels and length of study

Our specially-designed curriculum takes you from the English level you are at to the English level you need to achieve. We map you to our 8-level system, which aligns closely with other language learning standards including CEFR, IELTS and TOEFL.

Students can advance from one level to another in as little as four weeks, but typically progress at between eight to twelve weeks per level. Eight weeks is a sensible minimum to assume.

Program length guide

The best way to know how long you will need to study is to talk with an advisor, but you can get an idea here.

  1. GV1:
    Beginner
    • Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases aimed at the satisfaction of needs of a concrete type.
    • Can introduce yourself and others and can ask and answer questions about personal details such as where you live, people you know and things you have.
    • Can interact in a simple way provided the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help.
  2. GV2:
    Upper beginner
    • Can understand sentences and frequently used expressions related to areas of most immediate relevance (e.g. very basic personal and family information, shopping, local geography, employment).
    • Can communicate in simple and routine tasks requiring a simple and direct exchange of information on familiar and routine matters.
    • Can describe in simple terms aspects of your background, immediate environment and matters in areas of immediate need.
  3. GV3:
    Pre-intermediate
    • Can understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters regularly encountered in work, school, leisure, etc.
    • Can deal with most situations likely to arise while travelling in an area where the language is spoken.
    • Can produce simple connected text on topics that are familiar or of personal interest.
    • Can describe experiences and events, dreams, hopes and ambitions and briefly give reasons and explanations for opinions and plans.
  4. GV4:
    Lower intermediate
    • Text needed here.
  5. GV5:
    Intermediate
    • Can understand the main ideas of complex text on both concrete and abstract topics, including technical discussions in your field of specialisation.
    • Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity that makes regular interaction with native speakers quite possible without strain for either you or them.
    • Can produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects and explain a viewpoint on a topical issue giving the advantages and disadvantages of various options.
  6. GV6:
    Upper intermediate
    • Text needed here.
  7. GV7:
    Advanced
    • Can understand a wide range of demanding, longer clauses and recognise implicit meaning.
    • Can express ideas fluently and spontaneously without much obvious searching for expressions.
    • Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional purposes.
    • Can produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects, showing controlled use of organisational patterns, connectors and cohesive devices.
  8. GV8:
    Mastery
    • Can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read.
    • Can summarise information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation.
    • Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in the most complex situations.
